Volunteering with Us

Sunlove Home volunteers are a compassionate group of poeple constantly seeking happiness and interaction with the public at large. We provide a broad based opportunity for volunteers seeking to embark on a fruitful journey to give back to the society. If you are seeking for gratification and immense satisfaction towards your contribution and are interested in giving the time and skills that you have to helped the intellectually-disabled, then join us as a volunteer!
I volunteer at Sunlove Home regularly and I feel that I have learned so much from the people I have helped. - Elizabeth Lee, 26, regualar interaction volunteer
I help out in publicising Sunlove Home's programmes and it is very satisfying to see more people show interest in them. - John Yeo, 24, regular publicity volunteer

Enquiries

Is there an age limit for volunteering?

Yes, you will have to be 13 years old and above to volunteer with us. If you are found to have provided a false birth date in the volunteer form, we reserve the right to reject you.

What is the level of commitment needed for regular and programmes-based volunteers?

Regular Volunteers meet up with the director of Sunlove Home once a week and may also meet up outside of the official meeting time to discuss about the programmes or services we provide, depending on the committee they are in. However, most of the meetings are held in the evening, after most adults' working hours.

Programmes-based Volunteers will be contacted when there are special programmes coming up, such as courses or workshops. If they decide to help out in a particular programme, they will usually have to commit about at least one day per week to discuss the various aspects of the programme.

Can I sign up for more than one committee?

Yes. However, do ensure that you are able to cope with being in more than one committee.

Do I receive Values in Action (VIA) hours by volunteering at Sunlove Home?

Yes, please bring along your school's official VIA hours form/book/card if you would like to receive VIA hours for volunteering at Sunlove Home.

Is it possible to do a one-time class VIA at Sunlove Home?

Yes. However, the class will only be allowed to join the interaction committee because it is the only committee that is able to accommodate large groups of people.

Is Sunlove Home associated with any other organisations?

No. However, we do liaise with some other organisations which provide services for the intellectually disabled, just like we do. They include Silver Ribbon Singapore, Movement for the Intellectually Disabled of Singapore, and Singapore Association of Mental Health.

What do the various committees do?

The Administration Committee helps to collate the number of people coming for courses and workshops, and are also in-charge of doing up schedules and timetables when we provide our services such as health screenings. They also help ensure that we stay on track of time during programmes.

The Logistics Committee helps to prepare all materials needed for the programmes and ensures that items required for the services, such as the home assistance service, are brought to the location.

The Publicity Committee designs posters and come up with creative ideas to promote our programmes and services. Once they obtain the approval of the head committee of Sunlove Home, they will be able to proceed to execute their plan.

The Interaction Committee is in-charge of interacting with our beneficiaries. For instance, talking to the people at our daycare and rehabilation centres. They also help to serve food to the people at the centres and may even put up performances for them.
